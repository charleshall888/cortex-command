# Plan: 374

## Overview
Build the served next/advance lifecycle loop in five sequential, independently-shippable phases (each a complete increment once its predecessors are in; the phase boundary is the descope lever, not an order-independence claim): harden the event-writer substrate and add a claim/commit locking primitive (Phase 1); stamp a protocol handshake into existing verbs and add a skew evaluator + healer (Phase 2); ship the wheel-owned transition table plus `next`/`advance`/`describe` verbs (Phase 3); cut phase authority over to events-first with legacy artifact fallback and fold the two rogue transition-decision writers (Phase 4); rewire the interactive loop and write the roll-forward exit apparatus (Phase 5). Key architectural decisions are fixed by the spec: events.log stays the only durable state, the machine is a pure transition function, all emission is additive-only through one shared locked primitive, and the transition table is closed and wheel-owned.
**Architectural Pattern**: event-driven
