## Risks
- **2-task plan is deliberately coarse**: the change is a small, tightly-specified revert (2 files of substance + 1 regenerated mirror). The task boundaries are driven by hard coupling constraints, not arbitrary granularity: canonical + mirror MUST co-commit (drift hook fails closed on an unstaged mirror), and the budget + docstring edits share one file (same-file splits would race or create a fragile non-committing handoff). Splitting further would manufacture brittle intra-file handoffs without adding verification value.
- **No min-byte floor**: an accidental over-trim toward the rejected ~200B form would pass the *byte-count* gate but is now caught by Task 1 Verification (a) (empty `diff` vs the git-derived construction) and (c) (phrase-presence greps), promoting the deterministic-construction discipline from prose into executable checks. Review remains the final backstop.
- **Cross-skill coupling (semantic, not literal)**: `skills/discovery/SKILL.md`'s `when_to_use` disambiguates against research *behaviorally* ("research produces a research.md and stops") — it does NOT substring-match research's literal `research.md or conversation output` tail (`grep -Fc` of that literal in discovery = 0). So preserving the tail is required by the spec's R1, **not** by discovery, and this revert preserves it regardless. Flagged because the disambiguation is a semantic dependency with no test tripwire — a future change to research's *behavior* (not merely its wording) would silently desync discovery's claim.
