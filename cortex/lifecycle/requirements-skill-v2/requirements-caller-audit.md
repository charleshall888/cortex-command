## Enumerated argument shapes the v1 orchestrator supported

The thin-orchestrator rewrite MUST continue to dispatch correctly for each of these:

1. **Bare** — `/cortex-core:requirements` → project-scope gather + write.
2. **Literal `project`** — `/cortex-core:requirements project` → project-scope gather + write (alias of bare; appears in `references/gather.md:139` prose).
3. **Area slug** — `/cortex-core:requirements <area-kebab-case>` (e.g. `multiplayer`, `observability`) → area-scope gather + write against `cortex/requirements/{area}.md`.
4. **Reserved `list`** — `/cortex-core:requirements list` → read-only enumeration of existing requirements docs; does NOT enter the gather/write pipeline.

The `<scope>` placeholder in `references/gather.md:137` is the union of shapes 1/2 and shape 3 — no additional contract.

## Contract-preservation rules carried into the rewrite

- The frontmatter `description:` retains the three strings the trigger-phrase fixture asserts: `/cortex-core:requirements`, `gather requirements`, `define project scope`.
- The body explicitly enumerates the four shapes above so future callers (and the e2e test in Task 27) can grep for each.
- `list` is dispatched inline by the orchestrator (read directory, summarize) and does NOT invoke `/requirements-gather` or `/requirements-write`.
- Bare, `project`, and area-slug all route `/requirements-gather` → `/requirements-write` with the parsed scope value.
- The fenced sub-skill names (`/requirements-gather`, `/requirements-write`) appear in the orchestrator body so grep verification for Task 24 passes and downstream consumers (Task 27 e2e test) can detect the routing pair.
