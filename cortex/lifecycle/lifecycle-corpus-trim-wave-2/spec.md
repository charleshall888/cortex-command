# Specification: lifecycle-corpus-trim-wave-2

## Problem Statement

Every `/cortex-core:lifecycle` invocation loads 19–100KB of skill prose, much of it irrelevant to the active route: trunk runs load worktree machinery, finalize re-invocations load the PR-flow steps, every route loads both orchestrator-review checklists, and four delegation references always load together as separate files. The operator's target — confirmed by interview — is **~50% fewer per-run loaded tokens on common paths**, bought with route-conditional progressive disclosure, three wrapper verbs replacing always-loaded mechanical prose, reference merges, and bounded word-level compression. Corpus bytes fall as a byproduct. Refine, critical-review, commit, and pr are at re-verified audit floors and take only safe cuts; skills/research takes its first (~600B) dedup pass.

## Phases

- **Phase 1: Wrapper verbs** — build and test `cortex-lifecycle-enter`, `cortex-lifecycle-finalize`, `cortex-lifecycle-register-artifact`; registry, wrappers, pins.
- **Phase 2: Structural wave** — route-conditional extractions and splits, reference merges, prose rewiring onto the new verbs, test re-anchoring.
- **Phase 3: Compression and closeout** — checklist compression, word-level pass, sub-skill safe cuts, correctness fixes, mirrors, full-suite + load-path verification.

## Non-Requirements

- No functional scope cuts in critical-review (SHA-pin/sentinel machinery, A/B/C taxonomy, Straddle, rubric examples) or refine (injection sandwich, scar-tissue gotchas) — floors accepted by the operator.
- No L1 frontmatter ratchet changes; no budget-row raises.
- Corpus-bytes 50% is explicitly not the target; no cuts justified solely by disk-byte math.
- No new event names; no fourth ADR-0020 hand-written exemption; no backend logic inside verbs.
- The `--session-id null` dead-field inconsistency is out of scope (recorded as a backlog candidate, not fixed here).
- `cortex-update-item`, `cortex-generate-backlog-index`, `cortex-lifecycle-event`, `cortex-lifecycle-counters`, `cortex-lifecycle-init-ensure` binaries stay independently callable.

## Edge Cases

- **on_main Complete route**: first-run on-main short-circuits at Step 2 (skip Steps 2–4, jump to Steps 9–12, pr.json absent — no orphan probe); re-invocations go through Step 7, whose verb already classifies `on_main → Step 9`. The retained on-main note preserves the first-run short-circuit; the classifier is unchanged.
- **Enter verb on resume**: create-index is skip-if-exists; start-sync writes lifecycle-slug association only on `--phase none` — caller passes the resolver's phase; wrapper never re-derives new-vs-resume.
- **Enter verb, no backlog match**: `--backlog-file ""` propagates; `backlog_status: no_match`; no write-back skips silently beyond the backend gate.
- **Finalize re-run** (commit-retry, double invocation): idempotent guard matches parsed `event` field, not substring; no duplicate `feature_complete`.
- **Ambiguous slug at finalize**: update_item exit-2 equivalent propagates as exit 2 with candidates on stderr; skill applies the existing disambiguation rule.
- **Second-level reference read fails partially** (worktree-entry.md): end-to-end drive in R13 detects it; fallback is re-inlining §1a and keeping the smaller wins.
- **Corrupted events.log during any state read**: existing corrupted-state rule unchanged (treat as requiring review).
