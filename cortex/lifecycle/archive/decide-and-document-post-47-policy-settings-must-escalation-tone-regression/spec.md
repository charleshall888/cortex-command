# Specification: Decide and document post-4.7 policy settings (MUST-escalation, tone regression)

## Problem Statement

Two policy questions emerged from the post-Opus-4.7 harness audit (epic #82) that are durable norms about how cortex authors should write skill prompts, not technical-implementation decisions: (OQ3) when post-migration MUST escalation is observed, do we keep it or normalize back; (OQ6) do we add a warmth/tone directive to compensate for 4.7's documented voice regression, or accept the new voice. Today the OQ3 reconciliation ("default soft, escalate on observed failure") is inference-level only — research-stated, not a documented norm — and OQ6 is undecided. Without written policy entries, future skill edits re-debate these from scratch every lifecycle. This ticket writes both as durable contributor-facing norms in the repo CLAUDE.md so the discipline survives across authors and sessions.

## Edge Cases

- **Author cannot find an evidence artifact for an observed failure**: Per R2, MUST escalation is forbidden — the author keeps the soft form and either (a) raises effort per R3 or (b) accepts that the failure mode is not yet load-bearing enough to justify escalation. The policy provides no "soft observation accepted as evidence" loophole; if the author believes the failure is real but lacks an artifact, they FIRST run a session that produces an artifact (events.log entry, retro entry, transcript) and THEN escalate, in that order.
- **Effort-tuning infrastructure is itself unwired**: Per R3, the policy mandates citing the specific dispatch-path file and filing a wiring ticket — escalation to MUST as a workaround is explicitly forbidden by R3's wording.
- **CLAUDE.md crosses 100 lines during this edit**: Per R10, the edit must keep total size under 100 lines. If R10 fails verification post-edit, the implementation MUST compress text to fit (shorten cross-references, drop one example) — not cross the threshold and rely on R8's split-trigger to fire on the edit-after-this-one.
- **A new epic-82 policy ticket is filed after this one and crosses 100**: Per R8, that ticket triggers the split — extracts ALL policy entries (OQ3, OQ6, plus the new one) to `docs/policies.md`, leaves CLAUDE.md with a one-line pointer. The split is the new ticket's work.
- **Anchored preservation site from #85 is observed misfiring under 4.7**: Per R2, the over-fire is itself an artifact-bound observation; the author can reverse the preservation by softening, citing the observed failure as evidence. The policy works in both directions — escalation and de-escalation share the same evidence bar.
- **Overnight runner needs to add a MUST escalation**: The policy assumes interactive (daytime) diff review for verification per the project's day/night split philosophy. An overnight runner that identifies a need for MUST escalation MUST defer the addition: write the soft form with a `# TODO: OQ3 escalation candidate — evidence: <link>` comment in the diff, stop short of the MUST itself, and surface the candidate in the morning report. The user reviews and lands the MUST in a daytime interactive session if appropriate. Overnight is not blocked from skill edits — it is blocked from MUST additions specifically.
- **Future model release (4.8 or beyond) introduces new escalation guidance contradicting OQ3**: Per R8 trigger (a), this fires the OQ3 re-evaluation. The author reads R8 in CLAUDE.md, files a new "Decide and document" ticket under epic #82, and the new ticket either confirms or supersedes OQ3.
- **A skill author proposes a MUST without consulting the policy**: Enforcement is by reviewer (the user, in daytime diff review) — there is no automated gate. The reviewer reads the diff, looks for the artifact link in the commit body per R2, and rejects the escalation if the link is absent. The Non-Requirement on the linter explicitly leaves this as norm-level until breach is observed.
