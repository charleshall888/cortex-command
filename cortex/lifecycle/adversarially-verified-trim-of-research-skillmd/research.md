## Web Research

External authoring guidance is **strong and directly applicable** (not a thin topic), and largely confirms the project's existing stance.

- **Anthropic "Effective context engineering"** — the "right altitude" principle: avoid both over-specification ("brittle logic… increases maintenance complexity") and under-specification ("vague guidance… fails to give concrete signals"). "Minimal does not necessarily mean short." Method: start minimal, add back based on observed failure modes.
- **Anthropic Skill best-practices** (obra/superpowers mirror) — "the context window is a public good"; omit what Claude already knows. The **freedom-vs-fragility ladder** is the keep/cut test: low-freedom exact procedure only for *fragile/error-prone* steps; cut how-to on steps a capable model re-derives. Warns against deeply-nested references (Claude may only partially read chains).
- **Anthropic prompting best-practices (current models)** — **the MUST→positive-routing de-escalation is now official Anthropic guidance**, not just project convention ("Where you might have said 'CRITICAL: You MUST…', use 'Use this tool when…'"). "Prefer general instructions over prescriptive steps." "Tell Claude what to do instead of what not to do."
- **Prompting Opus 4.8** — **literal instruction-following**: 4.8 "does not silently generalize an instruction from one item to another… If you need Claude to apply an instruction broadly, state the scope explicitly." This is the dominant safe-trim hazard here (see Adversarial #8).
- **Prompt-bloat evidence** (Redis/MLOps) — longer prompts can be counterproductive; instruction-following weakens as context grows; "lost in the middle." Cite their *diagnosis* of bloat, not their runtime-RAG prescriptions.

### Anti-patterns to honor

- Do **not** import runtime prompt-compression (LLMLingua / token-merging) into a human-edited file — wrong layer.

## Tradeoffs & Alternatives

- **A — Full per-section adversarial classification** (ticket's literal approach): highest fidelity, mirrors precedent exactly. But cost is calibrated to a 12-file/195-proposal/40KB job; here the duplication axis is known-empty and the verbatim region's ceiling is ~0, so full classification + verification over the whole file spends the costliest pass on the lowest-yield region. Worst effort-to-yield.
- **B — Reuse the precedent's embedded analysis as the map**: cheapest; skips re-deriving the (none) duplication verdict and the verbatim exclusion. But the embedded map is **silent on How-narration** — which is the entire remaining trim — so B under-specifies the actual work.
- **C — Conservative defer-verbatim**: lowest risk; concentrates effort on the ~9.7 KB orchestrator-read prose. But "defer the whole 83-185 region" overreaches — the non-fenced scaffolding inside it is orchestrator-read and is exactly the classification the ticket asked for.

**Recommended: Hybrid — B as the starting map (reuse the no-duplication + verbatim-exclusion findings), classify the orchestrator-read prose including the non-fenced scaffolding within 83-185, preserve the fenced block *bodies* and the always-last ordering rule, and apply a single right-sized adversarial verification pass (not the 3-role panel) to each How-narration proposal.** Rationale: spends verification effort in proportion to risk and yield; the duplication axis is already adjudicated; the only genuinely new work is the How-narration pass; the heavyweight lock-in panel adds nothing over one careful verifier at this size (Adversarial #9).

5. **`test_skill_callgraph.py` reads every line** — run `just test`; the file is not test-invisible (low-probability rewrite hazard).
6. **`refine/SKILL.md:163` dangling-retarget is real and confirmed** — same-commit retarget required (see Rider #2).
7. **Canonicalization isn't free on hot paths** — the index.md six-bullet recipe (Rider #2) may be at correct altitude inlined; pressure-test before converting. The disjoint-Files concept (#3) is the stronger target.
