The team settled on a new `cortex init --ensure` CLI flag wired into the `/lifecycle` skill entry point only. The flag derives a content hash over the shipped template inventory, gitignore targets, CLAUDE.md fence version, and allowWrite shape, compares it against the hash in the per-repo `.cortex-init` marker, and dispatches to the existing init or update logic on mismatch — silently no-oping on match.

Four alternatives were considered. Silent SessionStart async writes were rejected on five concrete failure modes: contaminating foreign-repo clones, violating ADR-0003's write-surface contract, amplifying the CLAUDE.md no-flock race under concurrent sessions, expanding the force-push attack surface, and conflicting with SessionStart's documented context-injection purpose. SessionStart emitting additionalContext and relying on Claude to apply was rejected because additionalContext routing reliability is unmeasured and directive-routing escalation lacks the empirical evidence required to override the MUST-escalation policy default. Wiring all eight mutating skills was narrowed because drift symptoms concentrate at lifecycle entry and other skills tolerate slightly-stale scaffolding. A manually-bumped schema integer was dropped in favor of the content hash because a forgotten bump produces silent rot with no mechanical backstop.

The tradeoff accepted is coverage: users who invoke only discovery, backlog, refine, or other non-lifecycle skills on a drifted repo receive no auto-remediation. Missing gitignore entries in particular carry silent leak risk that no existing probe catches, leaving that exposure open until the user manually runs `cortex init --update` or a follow-up ticket widens the wiring.
