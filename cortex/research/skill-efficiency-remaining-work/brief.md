The audit settled on doing three small, targeted fixes rather than any large rewrite: pull the rarely-needed "competing plans" section out of the plan skill into a reference that only loads when a plan is actually flagged critical, fix a real bug in morning-review where an early step still auto-closes tickets before a merge is confirmed even though a later section already does this correctly post-merge, and move the dev-router's triage logic out of the main file so it only loads on the one routing path that actually uses it. Together these are framed as the leftover, lower-risk work remaining after an earlier offload effort already handled the bigger deterministic-procedure cleanups.

Several other options were on the table and didn't make the cut. Deduplicating repeated backend-routing prose across skills was rejected — those blocks aren't actually duplicates, they route different actions with site-specific logic, so collapsing them to a shared pointer would force extra reads and cost more than it saves. Migrating two event-emission call sites to a shared verb was left unresolved and is presumed dead, because one of the two writes its own record deliberately to stay comparable with the other for a downstream metrics consumer. Turning the dev-router triage logic into a deterministic CLI verb (instead of just relocating it) was rejected because that logic is judgment and presentation, not something that can be reduced to a deterministic procedure. And the most ambitious idea — giving each lifecycle phase a fresh, isolated context — was declined outright as too large and disruptive to the interactive lifecycle's step-by-step, human-approved flow.

The tradeoff: this approach explicitly leaves the deeper structural problem unsolved. Long interactive sessions still accumulate prose up to a real, measured ceiling, and the team is accepting that ceiling rather than re-architecting around it, on the judgment that the remaining cost is attention dilution rather than dollars, and that fixing it properly isn't worth the rewrite risk right now.
